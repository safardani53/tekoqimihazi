Netflix fans rave over 'hidden gem' film with epic Rotten Tomatoes score that left them 'crying thei

NETFLIX fans have been left distraught and devastated after watching one of the streamer's tear-jerking hidden gems.

Viewers of the emotional movie Miracle in Cell No.7 have claimed that the international film stirred feelings in them 'they hadn't felt in a long time.'

Netflix’s Turkish film, a remake of the 2013 Korean version of the same name, has skyrocketed in popularity, gaining a nearly perfect Rotten Tomatoes rating. 

The heart-breaking film follows the story of a mentally challenged man named Memo, played by Aras Bulut Iynemli, as he fights through the cruel Turkish legal system while imprisoned after he is wrongfully accused of murder. 

The film's logline reads: “Separated from his daughter, a father with an intellectual disability must prove his innocence when he is jailed for the death of a commander's child.”

A clip from the movie was posted on TikTok, and many people who watched the movie took to the comments section to praise the film.

One user described Miracle in Cell No.7 as an 'absolute hidden gem'.

They quipped that the film hadn't made them feel that highly emotional in 'a very long time'.

Another added: “Ok, so had to watch straight away... absolutely bawling.”

A third wrote: “Just watched it. OMG, tears dripping down my face. Absolute game changer. It’s totally brilliant. Thanks for the recommendation.”

A fourth penned: “I have seen this about three times, and I can’t stop crying, my kids don’t let me watch anymore.”

A fifth followed: “It's finished, and I'm still crying it's the most moving film I have EVER seen omg man, I wasn't ready for that.”'

The film, directed by Mehmet Ada Öztekin, starts with a bride-to-be getting emotional after hearing the news that capital punishment has been abolished in Turkey.

Shortly after the euphoric scenes, the film flashes back to 1983, where viewers see the bride, Ova, played by Nisa Sofiya Aksongur, in her younger years with her father, Mehmet 'Memo' Aras, who is living with a severe disability.

Ova and Memo reside in a small remote village with her grandmother, Fatma, played by Celile Toyon Uysal.

However, things quickly turn out for the worse, and the family are thrust into a challenging situation when Memo gets into an argument with the child of a military officer.

The loving father ends up innocently chasing the child after he sees that she has the bag Ova wanted.

Unfortunately, the officer's daughter loses her grip and falls off a cliff, leading to her death.

The child's powerful, furious, and grieving father throws Memo in jail and becomes hell-bent on sentencing Memo to death.

Whilst in prison, Memo - who is unaware of his actions due to his impairments - endures horrific, inhuman abuse.

As he starts serving his sentence in jail, his mother ends up dying of a heart attack after Ova takes it upon herself to go and visit her father, ultimately leaving the family home after penning a note for her grandmother to find.

Eventually, through many trials, the headstrong girl is smuggled into the prison, where she is reunited with her father in an attempt to convince his cellmates and prison wardens that he is, in fact, innocent and that everything has been a big misunderstanding.

Ova starts relaying to anyone who would listen that a deserter who had witnessed the alleged crime had been discovered and was willing to prove Memo's innocence.

Unfortunately, it was brushed aside as the dead girl's father became adamant about seeing someone pay for his daughter's death.

A fierce fight for freedom ensues as the little girl tries her utmost best for her small voice to be heard.
