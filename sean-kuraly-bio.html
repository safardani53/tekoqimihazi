Sean Kuraly – Early Life, Family & Education

In Dublin, Ohio, Sean Kuraly was born to his parents, Rick Kuraly and Jane Kuraly, on January 20, 1993. Furthermore, Rick was a star hockey player during his college days at the same university his son attended, Miami University.

In fact, Sean’s old man is the all-time leading goal-scorer and the third-highest points scorer in the Miami Redhawks‘ history.

On the other hand, his mother was a loving housemaker. Thus, having a father who was one of the best undoubtedly influenced young Sean to take up ice-hockey.

Aside from that, the 6 feet 2 inches centre grew up with his two brothers, Nick Kuraly and Chris Kuraly. Interestingly, both Nick and Sean play for the same franchise, the Boston Bruins.

Kurlay went to Cathedral Catholic High School, where he even earned Honor Roll.

The Ohio native played in the Quebec International Pee-Wee Hockey Tournament and the United States Hockey League (USHL) before college.

Likewise, Sean’s performances were so impressive during his rookie USHL season that he got drafted into the NHL in the 2011 NHL Entry Draft as an 18-year-old. 

However, Kuraly did not play in the NHL until 2016. Instead, the 27-year-old felt that he still needed some time to develop. Consequently, Sean decided to go to college for higher educations.

Talking about his education, Kuraly is a graduate of Miami University. During his four years in Miami, the 6 feet 2 inches centre played for the Miami Redhawks, where he performed admirably.

Professional Career

Initially, Sean’s NHL rights were with the San Jose Sharks as they drafted him as the 133rd overall pick in the 2011 NHL Entry Draft. However, the 27-year-old got traded to the Boston Bruins in 2015 for goaltender Martin Jones.

After that, Kuraly played for his college team for another year before making his professional debut with Boston’s AHL affiliate team, the Providence Bruins.

Subsequently, the Ohio native played one season for the franchise in which he scored 14 goals, 26 points, and assisted 12 times.

Thus having performed brilliantly, Sean was recalled to the Boston Bruins at the end of the 2016-17 season.

Thereafter, the 6 feet 2 centres made his debut against Tampa Bay Lightning in 2016, which they won in a 4-3 shootout victory.

Then, Kuraly’s first two career goals in the NHL came against Ottawa Senators in 2017. In doing so, he helped his side win the match 3-2 in overtime.

Moreover, it was in Game 5 of the Eastern Conference Quarterfinals, which made the occasion even more memorable.

Since then, Sean has been performing admirably well for the Bruins as their rotational player.

Furthermore, the 27-year-old has managed to appear a total of 154 times for the franchise. In those games, Kuraly has scored 14 goals, 36 points, and assisted 22 times.
