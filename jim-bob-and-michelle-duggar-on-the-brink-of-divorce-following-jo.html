On the Brink of Divorce Following Josh's Arrest?

Despite all that they’ve been through, Jim Bob and Michelle Duggar are still married.

That doesn’t mean, of course, that they’re happy.

Over the course of the past couple of years, we’ve heard all kinds of reports about how various members of the Duggar family are coping in the aftermath of Josh Duggar’s arrest on child pornography charges.

While Jinger Duggar has spoken out against her brother, and Jill wants the world to know she’s living her best life, for the most part, the family has been steering clear of social media, which probably means they’re having a difficult time.

That might not be out of sympathy for Josh, by the way.

The arrest and subsequent conviction has created professional crises for several Duggars, some of whom were left without any means of support following the cancelation of Counting On.

And among those who might be feeling concerned about their future earning potential is the patriarch himself, Jim Bob Duggar.

Not only has Jim Bob’s primary source of income been stripped away, he had the added financial burden of shelling out for Josh’s legal defense.

All of this while JB and wife Michelle continue to bear at least some responsibility for 18 other children, several of whom still live at home.

And there’s also a good chance that the damage to the family’s reputation will have a profound effect on Jim Bob’s other businesses, such as his used car dealership and his real estate company.

Not surprisingly, it seems that all of this has been putting quite a strain on Jim Bob and Michelle’s marriage.

“They aren’t as close as they once were because of all of Josh’s legal drama,” a source close to the situation tells People magazine.

“It’s creating a lot of stress on them — although the majority of the family is acting like nothing has happened.”

It seems the “pretend nothing happened” approach is usually Jim Bob’s favorite — but Michelle has made it clear that that’s not an option this time around.

“Jim Bob always wants to sweep things under the rug,” the insider said.

“He’s never going to talk about the scandal because it’s bad for business.”

And if Jim Bob is having trouble coping already, then he’s really gonna have a tough time in the months ahead.

Josh is serving 151 months in prison, and interest in the corruption and abuse within the Duggar clan is at an all-time high.

Apparently, JB feels the increased scrutiny could bring about the end of his business empire.

For once, Jim Bob might actually be right.

Prior to the trial, Jim Bob has encouraged Josh to accept a plea deal to minimize the bad press, but that didn’t happen.

If his parents’ marriage was strained already, then the trial — and the sguilty verdict — surely put their bonds to the test even more.

Divorce is strictly forbidden in the Duggars’ world, of course — but when they took their vows, we doubt Jim Bob and Michelle expected that they would ever face challenges like this.
