Pornhub was just BANNED on millions of devices around the world

ROKU is closing a well-known loophole that allows pornography to be shown on its devices.

The US streaming platform, which boasts more than 50million users, announced last week that it was shutting down so-called “non-certified channels” from March 2022.

That will effectively block apps like PornHub, which rely on the backdoor channels to stream X-rated content to users.

Roku already has rules in place that strictly prohibit the showing of pornography on its set-top boxes and smart TVs.

However, users got around the ban using non-certified channels, which allow developer's to stream anything they want to the platform.

These are mostly used by developers to test channels ahead of their launch on Roku, according to the California firm.

Adult entertainment apps use the system to skirt Roku's rules by setting up non-certified channels that host pornographic content.

The apps can then be added to users' Roku libraries using unique codes.

As well as PornHub, the impending change is expected to impact private channels such as Adult Time and Wicked, according to Protocol.

Users who add dodgy channels using this method are shown warning messages that the platform “does not test or review non-certified channels”.

The warning adds that some content may be “offensive or inappropriate for some audiences.”

The channels may be removed at any time, and a user's account may be blocked from loading other non-certified channels if the channel in question is illegal or violates Roku's terms.

The platform says that it is releasing an independent developer kit that means non-certified channels are no longer necessary.

Roku is a media player which enables you to stream digital content onto your television in a similar way to an Amazon Fire Stick.

While customers can buy set-top Roku boxes, the facility is also built into some TV sets. The service boasts 55million users in the United States.

The web-based service enables viewers to access some of the biggest internet streaming giants such as Netflix, Amazon Prime, Spotify and Hulu.

While it is legal to use services such as Netflix through Roku, as long as you pay for it, some cybercriminals are using the boxes to stream content illegally.

Some users take advantage of Roku’s “private channel functionality” to access sports and films without paying for them – which is illegal.

The system is also used to view pornography, which is strictly against Roku's terms of service.

The company has said it is committed to cracking down on piracy.
