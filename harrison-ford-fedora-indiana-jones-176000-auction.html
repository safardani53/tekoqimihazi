From Indiana Jones' hat to Harry Potter's wand - the film props set to make big bucks

A FEDORA worn by Harrison Ford in Indiana Jones and the Temple Of Doom is set to fetch up to $250,000.

An auction of Hollywood memorabilia next month could also see Daniel Radcliffe’s wand and specs from the Harry Potter And The Deathly Hallows films rake in up to $50,000 each.

Bids above $70,000 are likely for a remote-controlled R2-SHP droid from Star Wars: The Rise Of Skywalker.

Other items in the LA sale include a flute Sir Patrick Stewart played as Jean-Luc Picard in Star Trek: The Next Generation and a golf cart Brad Pitt used in Once Upon A Time...In Hollywood.

Organisers expect the rare items to fetch up to $6.2 million.

Brandon Alinger, Prop Store chief operating officer, said: "With more than 1,200 lots over three days, the auction includes amazing pieces from the past 60 years of film and television.
