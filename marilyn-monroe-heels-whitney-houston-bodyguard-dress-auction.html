MARILYN Monroe's heels, and an iconic Whitey Houston dress she wore in The Bodyguard are among 1,500 iconic Hollywood items that are up for grabs in a new auction, The U.S. Sun can reveal. 

The global auction, by Propstore, a vendor of original film and TV memorabilia, will hold its Entertainment Memorabilia Live Auction, from Thursday November 3rd to Sunday November 6th, with the full catalog live now. 

Among some of the big-ticket items are Marilyn Monroe's well-loved heels, which are from her foster sister's collection, named the Eleanor 'Bebe' Goddard Collection.  

The shoes are described as "black leather-and-suede high heels including ankle and foot straps embellished with small silver-toned studs."

The description continues: "The shoes are accompanied by a black-and-white photocopy of a portrait of Monroe, a Heaven and Earth Certificate of Authenticity, a Cooper Owen purchase invoice, and a printed Letter of Authenticity from Goddard, in which she mentions that the pair were "one of Marilyn's favorites." 

To walk a mile in Marilyn's shoes is expected to set you back around $6,000.

Whiteny Houston's iconic ensemble that she wore in The Bodyguard for her Queen of the Night performance, and in the subsequent music video, is expected to fetch much more than Marilyn's heels. 

The description reads "Rachel Marron (Whitney Houston) screen-matched complete Queen of the Night costume and original vac-formed lifecast from Mick Jackson's romantic thriller The Bodyguard.  

"Rachel wore her costume when she performed the song Queen of the Night in a nightclub. The performance was halted after fans stormed the stage and Frank (Kevin Costner) carried Rachel to safety – the iconic image featured on the poster for the movie. The Bodyguard was Whitney Houston's acting debut." 

With the lot, the lucky bidder will also get an original vacuum-formed plastic lifecast of Whitney Houston's face presented on a custom-made stand.

This casting was used for costume fitting and the headdress included in this lot fits the lifecast exactly. Completing the lot is a sealed vinyl edition of The Bodyguard soundtrack and two stills from the film. 

The Whitney Houston Queen of the Night lot is expected to bring in between $45,000 and $68,000. 

Some other notable items include Michelle Pfeiffer's Catwoman cowl production master and Gal Gadot's Wonder Woman tiara.  

Bids can take place online, by phone or in person at BAFTA 195 in Piccadilly, London.
