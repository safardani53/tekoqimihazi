GABBY'S JOURNEY

The former Denver Broncos cheerleader was one of two bachelorettes in season 19 of The Bachelorette.

She ended the season with a fiancé, Erich Schwer, from the show, but they announced on November 4, 2022, that they'd called it quits.

Gabby is originally from O'Fallon, Illinois but moved to Colorado, where she was hired as a cheerleader for NFL's Denver Broncos.

She cheered for the team from 2016 through the end of the 2019-2020 season.

However, aside from her rise as a well-known reality TV personality, Gabby worked as a nurse for three years before joining the Denver Broncos in 2016.

She used her experience during the Covid-19 pandemic and her nursing experience to assist where hospitals were short-staffed and in dire need of help.

During that time, she worked 12 hours or more. She won the Pop Warner Humanitarian Award in 2020– alongside the Cleveland Chiefs lineman Laurent Duvernay-Tardif – making her the first woman to receive the award.

Typically, only NFL players receive the award.

Pop Warner Little Scholars released a press statement at the time: "Windey and Duvarney-Tardif's selflessness and commitment to helping others are an embodiment of sacrifice and teamwork, making them model representatives for young student-athletes across the nation."

She moved on to reality TV when she appeared as a contestant on The Bachelor, where she competed for Clayton Echard's heart.

After Echard proposed to another woman, Gabby went on to find love as one of two bachelorettes on season 19 of The Bachelorette.

She ended the season with an engagement to Erich Schwer. She spoke about her family life while on the show, revealing she isn't close to her mom.

However, less than two months after the season ended, E! News reported that they were calling off their engagement.

"She felt they weren't aligned and weren't on the same page," the insider told E!

"Gabby was hoping they could give each other space and work it out but ultimately decided they didn't have a future."

BACHELORETTES

Gabby appeared alongside Rachel Recchia in season 19 of The Bachelorette.

It was the first time two women appeared on the show simultaneously, and the men could pick which woman they wanted to pursue.

This was not without some drawbacks, as contestant Logan Palmer told Rachel he was switching to pursue Windey instead.

He told Rachel: "I have feelings for Gabby…. I'm going to have to step away from pursuing things with you."

In an Instagram post, Logan apologized to Rachel and wrote: "I'm sorry that I added more difficulty to an already challenging journey.

"That was never my intention, but it was inspiring how strong you were through it all."

Windey did eventually choose Erich Schwer, who got down on one knee in the season finale despite him expressing his doubts in the previous episode.

"I think I want to date you," Erich told Gabby after hometown, confessing he wasn't sure if he was ready to propose.
