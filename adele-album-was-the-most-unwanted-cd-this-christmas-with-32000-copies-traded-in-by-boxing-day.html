Adele album was the most unwanted CD this Christmas with 32,000 copies traded in by Boxing Day

ADELE’S third studio album 25 was Christmas’s most unwanted CD.

Second-hand website musicmagpie.com said more than 32,000 copies of the album had been traded in by Boxing Day.

It was followed by Justin Bieber’s Purpose, with just over 25,000 copies.

The Now! 93 compilation, One Direction's Made In The AM and Coldplay's A Head Full Of Dreams also made the top five.

Disney cartoon Frozen was the most unloved DVD, with nearly 26,000 copies traded in, followed by 24,563 copies of James Bond movie Spectre.

The least wanted mobile was the iPhone 6s.

According to the website, the figures mark a record high for products sold on over the two-day Christmas period.

The company said it bought more then 2.5 million unwanted items, with 10,000 tech items alone reaching a combined worth of £650,000.
