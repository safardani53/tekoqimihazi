Ethan Coen (Director) - Age, Birthday, Bio, Facts, Family, Net Worth, Height &amp; More

Joel Coen and Ethan Coen, collectively known as the Coen brothers, are an American director, producer, screenwriter and publisher. Their movies span a wide variety of genres and styles, which they often sabotage or parody. Their most acclaimed works include: Raise Arizona, Miller's Crossing, Fargo, The Big Lebowski, O Brother, Where Art Thou?, No Country for Old Men, Burn After Reading, A Serious Man, True Grit, Inside Llewyn Davis and The Ballad of Buster Scruggs. The brothers wrote, directed and produced their films together, though until The Ladykillers Joel received sole credit for directing and Ethan for production. They often rotate the best payout for their scripts while sharing editing credits under the Roderick Jaynes alias. Together they were nominated for 13 Academy Awards and each received one, bringing in Best Original Screenplay for Fargo and Best Film, Best Director and Best Adaptation Screenplay for No Country for Old Men.
