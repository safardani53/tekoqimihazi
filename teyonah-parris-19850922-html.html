Teyonah Parris is an American actress. She began her career with the lead role in the TV series AMC Mad Men and starred in the 2014 independent film Dear White People. Since then, Parris has starred in Spike Lee's Chi-Raq, American soap opera Empire, and the film nominated by the academy If Beale Street Could Talk. In 2021, Parris had a breakthrough role when playing the role of mature Monica Rambeau in the Marvel Cinematic Universe Disney + WandaVision series. Thanks to her performances, she won two Black Reel awards and received nominations for the Screen Actors Guild Awards and the NAACP Image Awards.
