Trader Joe's confirms it discontinued fan-favorite product - and customers are 'appalled'

TRADER Joe's has confirmed its customer-favorite seasoning has been discontinued.

The grocery giant told The U.S. Sun that its Vegan Chicken-less Seasoning is gone for good.

The seasoning salt contained onion powder, garlic powder, turmeric, and salt and was free from gluten and MSG.

Although the seasoning itself was vegan, many customers added the product to vegan and non-vegan foods alike, including miso soup, noodles, salads, mashed potatoes, grilled chicken, quinoa, black beans, tofu, mixed greens, fajitas, and veggie burgers.

It remains unclear how long the seasoning has been gone. However, fans started noticing the seasoning was missing from shelves in 2021.

Disappointed customers have taken to social media to beg the company to bring it back.

One user wrote on Twitter: "Just finding out that Trader Joe’s discontinued their vegan chickenless seasoning salt and I am APPALLED."

Fans have even gone as far as to start a petition online collecting hundreds of signatures.

SAYING SAYONARA

Over the years, Trader Joe's has discontinued many products, leaving customers without their favorites.

Recently, the favored grocery chain axed its Multigrain Waffles - a healthier twist on the iconic breakfast treat.

Most read in Money

Trader Joe's didn't disclose what led to the multigrain waffles’ discontinuation however many products are discontinued due to supply challenges or customer complaints.

Plus, an employee told The U.S. Sun that despite the multigrain waffles being gone, shoppers can expect a new version to hit stores in just days.

The employee said that it won’t be the exact product customers know and love, and it will be called “Whole Grain Waffles” instead of multigrain.

Another product customers have had to say goodbye to is the vegan ranch crunch salad.

This was a kit that included green cabbage, romaine lettuce, kale, fried onions, roasted sunflower kernels, carrots, and green onions.

Another item off the shelves is Tabbouleh Style Hummus which had chunks of tomato, parsley, onion, mint, and lemon.

SAVING AT TRADER JOE'S

Despite Trader Joe's not having a loyalty program, it still offers low prices.

"We have low prices, every day. NO coupons. NO membership cards. NO discounts. NO glitzy promotions or couponing wars at our stores. We offer the best everyday values, every day," Trader Joe's said on its website.

While Trader Joe's doesn't carry coupons either, it does accept manufacturer's coupons for other products they have.

A manufacturer coupon is a coupon issued by the manufacturer offering a free item or a specific discount on the purchase price of the manufacturer's products.

They are quite rare, so if you are able to find one, cherish it.

In order to see what's new or possibly receive any discount at Trader Joe's, customers must sign up for their newsletter.

In the newsletter, patrons can hear about new deals, possibly collect any coupons, or even pick up their Frequent Flyer to hear about current deals.
