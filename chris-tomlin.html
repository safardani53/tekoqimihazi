Chris Tomlin Biography

Christopher Dwayne Tomlin is contemporary Christian music (CCM) artist, worship leader, and songwriter from the United States of America.

Chris Tomlin Early Life, Education

He was born on 4 May 1972 in Grand Saline, Texas to Connie and Donna Tomlin. He is an elder brother to Ryan and Cory. Tomlin learned guitar from Willie Nelson records and wrote his first worship song at the age of fourteen.

He attended Tyler Junior College as well as Texas A&M University. He was a worship leader in various church camps in the mid-1990s.

Career & Awards

Tomlin was nominated for Grammy awards seven times and won Best Contemporary Christian Music Album for "If Our God Is For Us" in 2012. He has a record of nominations for Dove Awards 32 times and holds the title 19 times.

He is active on Twitter and Instagram. His first album was "noise", which was released in 2001 that succeeded to rank at the top position on the worship song lists. Although Tomlin is a solo artist, he also uses a band lineup and plays guitar as well as piano for his band.

Tomlin's five songs, that he wrote himself were included in Christian Copyright Licensing International's list of the top 25 worship songs in the US in August 2007. In May 2008 he left The Austin Stone Community Church, to start a new church in Atlanta, Georgia in which he was one of the worship leaders.
