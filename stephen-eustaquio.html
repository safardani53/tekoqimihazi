Career

  • Eustáquio, who was born in Leamington, Ontario to Portuguese parents, relocated to Portugal when he was seven years old. After starting off in amateur soccer with Nazarenos, he played two seasons in the third level with Torreense.
  • Eustáquio joined Leixes on June 7, 2017, for an unknown amount. On 23 July, he made his professional debut in a 2-0 home victory over Académico de Viseu in the first round of the Taça da Liga, where he played the entire 90 minutes, and his debut in the Segunda Liga occurred on 6 August, in a 4-1 away defeat to Real Massamá.
  • Eustáquio signed a five-and-a-half-year contract with Chaves after his €500,000 buyout clause was met. Four days later, he made his Primeira Liga debut, playing the full 2-1 away win against Feirense.
  • On 14 September 2018, in a Portuguese League Cup encounter at the Estádio do Drago, Eustáquio scored a late equalizer to assist Chaves to draw 1-1 and earn their first-ever point at Porto’s home field.
  • Eustáquio joined Cruz Azul of the Mexican Liga MX on January 15, 2019. Vtor Severino, who served as an assistant to manager Luis Castro at Chaves, characterized him as “the classic player from La Masia, from Barcelona.” On his debut, against Tijuana, he was sent off only minutes after entering the field as a replacement, but the video assistant referee ultimately reprimanded him with just a yellow card after a review; nonetheless, he was stretchered off with an injury soon after.
  • Eustáquio returned to the field on September 22, 2019, after being out for eight months, playing with the under-20 squad in preparation for his complete comeback.
  • Eustáquio was loaned to Portuguese top-flight team Paços de Ferreira for the rest of the season in December 2019. He made his league debut on January 11, 2020, in a 0-0 away draw against Portimonense.
  • Eustáquio agreed to another loan in September 2020.
  • The transfer was made permanent in January of the following year for a cost of €2.5 million. On 24 October, he scored his first goal for them, completing a 1-1 league draw against Nacional. On 5 August 2021, his European debut, he scored the third goal in a 4-0 home victory against Larne in the third qualifying round of the UEFA Europa Conference League.
  • Eustáquio was loaned to Porto for the remainder of the season in January 2022, with an option to purchase. On February 6, he made his debut, replacing Fábio Vieira late in a 2-0 away loss against Arouca.
  • On May 31, 2022, Porto exercised its purchase option, signing Eustáquio to a deal until June 30, 2027.
  • Eustáquio, who is eligible to play for both Canada and Portugal, represented Canada’s under-17 team at the 2012 AGS Cup. Rui Jorge, the manager of Portugal’s under-21 team, chose him for the 2019 UEFA European Championship qualifiers against Romania and Switzerland, which were scheduled for early November. He earned his first cap against the former, playing 90 minutes and getting booked in a 1-1 draw in Constanța.
  • Eustáquio agreed to play for Canada in February 2019. When he returned from his knee injury, he got his first call-up to the squad on 1 October 2019 for a CONCACAF Nations League match against the United States. He made his debut on November 15th, coming off the bench for Mark-Anthony Kaye in the second half of the 4-1 away loss.
  • Eustáquio was nominated to a 60-man preliminary team for the 2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup on June 18. He was named to the tournament’s final squad on July 1. He scored his first international goal in the opening group game, a 4-1 victory against Martinique in Kansas City.
