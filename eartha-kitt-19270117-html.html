Eartha Kitt (Pop Singer) - Age, Birthday, Bio, Facts, Family, Net Worth, Height &amp; More

Eartha Kitt is an American singer, actor, comedian, dancer, activist and author known for his distinctive singing style and his 1953 recording of “It's So Good” and the new Christmas song “Santa Baby”, who have both peaked at 10 on the Billboard Hot 100. Orson Welles once called her "the most interesting woman in the world." Kitt began her career in 1942 and appeared in the original Broadway Theater musical Carib Song in 1945. In the early 1950s, she had six American Top 30 hits, including "Uska Dara" and "I Want to Be Evil ". Her other notable recordings include the British hits "Under the Bridges of Paris", "Just an Old Fashioned Girl" and "Where Is My Man". She played the role of Catwoman in the third and final season of the 1967 Batman television series. In 1968, her career in the United States failed after making statements against the Vietnam War during a lunch at the House. White.
