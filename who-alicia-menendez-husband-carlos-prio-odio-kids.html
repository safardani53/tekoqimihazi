Who is Alicia Menendez's husband, Carlos Pro Odio?

MSNBC'S Alicia Menendez is widely known for her weekend show American Voices with Alicia Menendez.

Supporting her throughout her growing career is her husband, Carlos Prío Odio, and here's everything we know about their relationship.

Who is Carlos Prío Odio?

Carlos Prío Odio is a Donor Advisor and co-founder for EquisLabs based in Miami, Florida.

After graduating from the American University, Carlos worked as the Deputy Latino Vote Director for Obama for America in 2007.

He then went on to work as the Deputy Associate Director at The White House's Office of Political Affairs from 2009 until 2011.

Carlos has held multiple positions since before becoming the co-founder of EquisLabs.

According to his LinkedIn, EquisLabs works "to fill gaps in our understanding and engagement of the Latino electorate."

How did Carlos and Alicia meet?

Carlos and Alicia knew of each other before officially meeting.

While working as a freelance journalist in December 2008, Alicia had hopes of interviewing Carlos for her article on Hispanic voters.

Instead, he refused to answer her questions. In fact, the two didn't get along until they were face to face.

It wasn't until September 2009 that Carlos and Alicia officially met at a reception at the National Museum of American History.

Though at the time, Alicia was seeing someone else, when the two caught each other's eye, it was love at first sight.

Speaking with the NY Times, Carlos said: "I turned around and saw this stunning creation."

Eventually, the two ended up being single, and shortly after Carlos and Alicia began dating.

By September 2013, the two were engaged and married two years later.

Do Alicia and Carlos have kids?

Alicia and Carlos have welcomed two kids together.

On December 15, 2016, the longtime couple welcomed their first child, a daughter named Evangelina.

On August 20, 2019, Carlos and Alicia welcomed their second daughter Ofelia.

The two often share pictures of their family on holidays.
