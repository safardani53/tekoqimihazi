Who is Sandra Smith's husband John Conolly?

SANDRA Smith's husband John Conolly is 12 years older than his TV anchor wife.

Fox News personality Sandra credits John for introducing her to his passions which include art and sailing.

Who is John Conolly?

John Conolly is the husband of Fox News American Reports co-anchor Sandra Smith. 

In 2016, he appeared on Fox & Friends with his wife as they cooked Super Bowl chili.

Conolly has a Bachelor of Fine Arts in Painting from the School of Art Institute in Chicago but currently works in finance.

His LinkedIn profile has stated he is self-employed since 2017.

His other interests include cooking and sailing.

Sandra told the Chicago Tribune that he was “a very competitive sailor” when they first started dating and took her sailing several times.

“He was challenging me; I wasn’t going sailing and to art shows when I met him,” Sandra admitted.

How did he meet Sandra?

John and Sandra met on her first day working as a sales trader at Terra Nova Institutional in Chicago.

John, who was 35 years old at the time, said it was "love at first sight” when he was introduced to a 23-year-old Sandra.

He encouraged her to get into television commentary.

When he left the company to start his own business just weeks after they met, she took over as the chief TV commentator at the firm.

The couple had a long-distance relationship for several years after Sandra moved to New York for work where she eventually joined Fox News in 2007.

They married in May 2010 and John remained in Chicago until January 2011 until joining his wife in New York City.

Do they have children?

John and Sandra have two children.

Their daughter Cora was born in 2013 and their son John Jr was born shortly after in 2015.

While John doesn’t post much on social media, Sandra has shared pictures on Instagram of their children as well as their dog namedWhiskey.
