Canvey Island golf course blighted by violent thugs tells members they must play at their 'own risk'

GOLFERS are being warned they use a course at their own risk after police failed to stop yobs.

Bosses have closed the club house and withdrawn all staff “until further notice”.

They say players and workers have been threatened with blades and metal poles, while bikers use fairways and greens for scrambling at Castle Point Course in Canvey Island, Essex.

A sign at the clubhouse says “an increased number of incidents of anti social behaviour" have given "us concerns towards staff and customer safety and welfare."

It adds: "A number of incidents have included threats and acts of physical assault towards staff members and customers including two in the past two days.

“It is with regret that Glendale Golf are therefore taking the decision to remove all staffing from the golf course and clubhouse with immediate effect and until further notice.

“We are speaking with Castle Point Borough Council and the local police on the matter and we will re-assess the situation once we are confident that greater levels of security are available to reduce the number of issues and the likelihood of a serious incident.

“The golf course will remain available for use to all members and customers so please feel free to continue to play golf at Castle Point if you wish to do so.

“We do advise that this is done so at your own risk and please also take into consideration that the normal levels of maintenance will not be kept and the clubhouse will not be open until staff return to work.”

Maintenance worker Paul Estlea, 52, of Landgon Hills, Essex, said closing the clubhouse was “an invite for the yobs to come down."
