Career

  • From 2010 through 2013, Kuhlman played for Cloquet High in the Minnesota State High School League (USHS). The Dubuque Fighting Saints picked him with the 48th overall pick in the 2011 USHL Futures Draft.
  • He played in five games with the Fighting Saints during the 2011–12 season, then played in the USHL and high school hockey the following year during the 2012–13 campaign before joining the Dubuque for the whole postseason in their ultimate Clark Cup victory.
  • In his one full season with the Dubuque Fighting Saints in 2013–14, Kuhlman had 44 points—25 goals and 19 assists—in 56 matches. He was also chosen to represent the USHL in the All-Star Game. He made a commitment to play college hockey for the NCAA’s National Collegiate Hockey Conference’s Bulldogs men’s ice hockey team at the University of Minnesota Duluth (NCHC).
  • Kuhlman contributed 8 goals and 10 assists for 18 points in his Bulldogs’ rookie year in 2014–15, helping the club secure an NCAA Tournament berth. He had early leadership potential and was an alternate captain in the 2015–16 season as a sophomore while tallying 12 goals and 20 points in 40 games.
  • Kuhlman had a productive junior year in 2016–17, helping the Bulldogs reach the Frozen Four before losing to the University of Denver in the NCAA Championship game with 22 points in 42 games (a career-best). Kuhlman, who is approaching his last undergraduate season, was named team captain for the 2017–18 campaign.
  • Kuhlman joined the Boston Bruins on April 11, 2018, after being undrafted in all NHL Entry Drafts and winning a championship with the Bulldogs. His contract was for two years and was an entry-level one. To finish the 2017–18 season, he promptly signed an amateur try-out deal with the Providence Bruins of the American Hockey League (AHL).
  • As a two-way forward, Kuhlman continued to contribute, scoring 13 goals and accumulating 20 points in 44 games. He led the Bulldogs to a 2-1 win against the University of Notre Dame in the NCAA Championship, earning him the title of 2018 NCAA Tournament MVP. With 166 straight college games played, he finished his collegiate career eighth all-time in the NCAA.
