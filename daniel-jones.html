Daniel Jones | Early Life

Starting with the basic detail. Daniel Jones was born on May 27, 1997, to his parents, Becca and Steve Jones, in Charlotte, North Carolina. He grew up with his siblings Becca Jr, Bates, and Ruthie.

Daniel gets along quite well with his siblings, and they look up to each other in a certain aspect.

And during his childhood days, he was a huge fan of Eli Manning, the formal football quarterback in the NFL, who played fifteen years for the New York Jets.

He dreamed of becoming a quarterback in the NFL since his third standard.

Family Line

Daniel belongs to a family where everyone is related to sports. Since the beginning, they had a family rule that no matter what, one has to attend sports events as much as possible.

Becca is the oldest and is 26 years old, named after her mother.

She played hockey for four years at Davidson College. She is as playful and skillful as her mother was in playing hockey.

Bates, Daniels’s brother, is 25 years old in 2023. He has enrolled on the men’s basketball team, serving as a forward at Denver College.   

Ruthie is the youngest and jolliest kid in the family. She also follows Daniel at Duke University. She is a soccer player and was also titled to the U.S. Soccer site for the Under-15 Girls’ National team.

Daniel Jones | High School Career

He went on to attend Charlotte Latin School in Charlotte, North Carolina. During high school, he gathered 6,997 passing yards and 98 touchdowns.

It was a normal and average score, but colleges hardly hired him.

Daniel later went to Princeton University but eventually attended Duke University to play college football.

Daniel Jones | College Career

As he was transferred according to the rules, he was redshirted for the first year at Duke University.

Daniel got to play as a sophomore only when Thomas Sirk, the quarterback, suffered an injury in 2016.

Daniel kicked off the season well, scoring sixteen touchdowns and nine interceptions, completing 270 passes.

His scorings for touchdowns and interceptions increased to 14 and 11 for his junior year.

This must be his first career-winning highlight. Daniel was titled the MVP of the postseason college football bowl game, the 2017 Quick Lane Bowl. He was competing against Northern Illinois.

One more achievement was added to his list during his senior year.

Daniel was upgraded to MVP title in the 2018 Independence Bowl, where he yielded 423 passing yards and five touchdowns against the 2018 Temple Football team.

He also received the MVP award for the 2019 Senior Bowl game.

Daniel Jones | Professional Career

The New York Giants drafted him with the sixth overall pick in the first round of the 2019 NFL Draft.

Well, clearly, it has not even crossed two years of his professional life as he made his official debut on August 8, 2019, against the inter-league rival New York Jets.

The most interesting phase of his life would be when he got to play as a backup behind the man he looked up to.

You must have a hint by now because the man is Eli Manning. He got to play his first regular season against Dallas.

Daniel was titled the starter of the game against the Tampa Bay Buccaneers in Week 3.

He and his team bet on the Buccaneers and became the seventh rookie in NFL history to have the game-winning urge.

Daniel became the NFC Offensive Player of the Week in Week 3. He gave his team two regular wins against Washington Redskins and Buccaneers.

He received the Pepsi Offensive Rookie of the Week in the 16th Week for his brilliance as a rookie player.

After 2020

Coming to 2020, Daniel became the team captain for his second season. Before this, Eli Manning earned that honor and responsibility for thirteen years.

It seems like Daniel was seriously following in his idol’s footsteps.

Daniel had to face a hamstring injury in Week 12 against the Cincinnati Bengals, leading him to miss the rest of the games.

His coach, Joe Judge, showed optimism, but we must wait for Daniel to respond to recover as soon as possible.

Jones suffered a 27–13 defeat to the Denver Broncos at the beginning of the 2021 season. 

He attempted for a career-high 402 yards, two touchdowns, and an interception in the Giants’ 27–21 overtime victory over the New Orleans Saints.

He was named NFC Offensive Player of the Week for his performance.

Daniel threw for 203 yards, one touchdown, and zero interceptions in Week 7 against the Carolina Panthers. He also rushed for 28 yards and caught the first pass of his career from Dante Pettis for 16 yards in the 25–3 victory.

Jones suffered a neck injury in Week 12 against the Philadelphia Eagles, requiring him to miss the remainder of the season. Jones was placed on injured reserve on December 20, 2021.

In 2022

In April 2022, the Giants rejected the fifth-year option of Jones for 2023.

He performed against the Tennessee Titans for the opening season, 21–20 in the comeback win.

Jones competed against the Green Bay Packers with 21 passes of 27 attempts for 217 yards and rushed for 37 yards with 10 carries in an upset win in Week 5.

He accomplished the 23-17 win against the Jacksonville Jaguars in Week 7 since Frank Filchock in 1946.

Jones threw for 202 yards and a touchdown and rushed for 107 yards becoming the first Giants QB to record 100 rush yards in a game.

Jones was named NFC Offensive Player of the Week for Week 7 due to his performance.
