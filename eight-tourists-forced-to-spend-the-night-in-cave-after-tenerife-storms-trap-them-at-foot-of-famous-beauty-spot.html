Eight tourists forced to spend the night in CAVE after Tenerife storms trap them at foot of famous b

TOURISTS hoping for a sunshine holiday in Tenerife spent the night in a cave after ferocious storms battered the Canary Islands.

The eight holidaymakers became stranded last night but rescuers were unable to reach them through five-metre waves and 75 mph winds.

It was considered too dangerous to try and walk down the mountain at night or to use the emergency helicopter and the sea was too rough for a boat rescue.

The tourists, understood to be German, are stranded at the foot of a 200 ft ravine known as the Masca Barranco, a well-known but challenging beauty spot near Los Gigantes.

It has been the scene of numerous rescues because even experienced hikers can get caught out by the sheer walk.

It takes about three hours to get down to the sea and the only way out is either by boat or to climb back up again.

The emergency services are currently working out how to get to the stranded tourists who would have had no shelter last night except for finding the cave. They are not thought to be injured.

It is believed a local shepherd was on hand to help them.

The hamlet of Masca, with houses perched on top of the cliffs, is the second most visited natural enclave of Tenerife after Mount Teide.

Firefighters recently warned about the difficulties of the ravine walk down to the sea and there have previously been several deaths and numerous rescues.

The Canary storms also brought torrential rain which caused extensive flooding across the islands and at one stage, both aiports on Tenerife were closed yesterday for nearly two hours.

Dozens of flights were affected across the Canaries but they are now operating as normal.

In Tenerife, around 900 holidaymakers couldn't get home last night and were put up in hotels.

Emergency services had to deal with hundreds of incidents including landslides, fallen branches, trees on road, demolished road signs and power cuts.
