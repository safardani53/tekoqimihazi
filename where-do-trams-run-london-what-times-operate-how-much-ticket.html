Where do trams run in London, what times do they operate and how much is a ticket?

THE London tram network is called Tramlink and runs in South London.

The trams were introduced in 2000 and have proved a popular way to travel.  They are treated as part of the bus network and all trams have step-free access.

Where do the trams run?

The trams run through central Croydon from Wimbledon, Beckenham, Elmers End and New Addington.

The Tramlink has 39 stops and spans over 18 miles with a fleet of 34 trams.

The centre of the routes loop through Croydon town centre and the trams are part of the Transport for London network.

What times do the trams run?

Trams run every 10 minutes in the daytime on Mondays to Saturdays on all routes except to New Addington, where they run every seven minutes.

These combined routes means a tram runs through Croydon town centre every 1-6 minutes.

Each of the routes run a different timetable, but generally all three start between 5am and 6am and run right through to finish after 1am.

Can I use my Oyster on Tramlink?

Yes you can use your Oyster card on the trams.

However you only touch in at the starting tram stop. Unlike tube rides, a touch out isn't required at the end of your journey (except at Wimbledon).

How much is a tram ticket?

A tram ride costs £1.50 if you pay with contactless or Oyster.

If you buy a single paper ticket from a machine at a tram stop, it will cost £2.60.

A Hopper fare applies on the tram network - if you use your Oyster card for two journeys within an hour, you get the second journey free.

How do trams work?

Trams are just over 30 metres long and are powered via overhead electrical wires along purpose built tracks.

They have a maximum speed of around 50mph and each tram can carry over 200 people.
