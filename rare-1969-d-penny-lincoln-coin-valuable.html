Rare 1969 D penny Lincoln coin sells for more than $2,000 - and you could have the same one in your

COIN collectors could make a mint if they spot a rare penny in their wallet, with one coin recently selling for $2,010 on eBay.

The circulated one cent coin is a so-called D penny from 1969, featuring 16th President Abraham Lincoln.

The coin comes with the text "In God We Trust" alongside the top of the piece, and "Liberty" on the left hand side.

Below the year 1969 on the right hand side, it also features the letter "D", which means it was minted in Denver.

This is what makes the coin different to the Lincoln Memorial and Wheat coins, which were both minted in Philadelphia.

These coins recently sold for $325 and $1,801 respectively, but they bear no mintmark.

More than four million coins are said to have been minted by the Denver Colorado Mint in 1969.

The specific coin attracted 25 bids from keen coin collectors and then sold for $2,010 to the winner on Sunday, October 10.

If you happen to spot one in your wallet or down the back of your sofa, keep in mind that the coin is only worth what someone is willing to pay.

In other words, you're not guaranteed to get thousands if you have a similar one.

The 1969 D penny coins are worth around $12 if they're in a mint certified state, according to Cointrackers.

This may not sound like a lot, but it's still 119,900% its original value.

Is your small change worth a fortune?

If you believe that you have a rare coin then you may be able to make a real mint.

The most valuable coins are usually those with a low mintage or an error, as they're deemed the most valuable by collectors.

To find out if your small change could be worth a fortune, check how much the specific coins are selling for on eBay.

Search the full name of the coin, select the "sold" listing and then toggle the search to "highest value".

It will give you an idea of the amount of money that the coin is going for.

You can either choose to sell the coin on eBay or through a specialist site such as Coinappraiser or USA Coin Book.

If you choose eBay, remember to set a minimum price that is higher or at the very least equal to the face value of the coin - or you'll make a loss.

You also need to be aware that even if your coin “sells” on eBay for a high price there’s no guarantee that the buyer will pay up.

In its terms and conditions, eBay states that bidders enter a “legally binding contract to purchase an item”, but there’s no way to enforce this rule.

The most eBay can do is add a note to their account for the unpaid item or remove their ability to bid and buy.
