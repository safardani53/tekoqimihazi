Sarah Jones, Mac Jones Sister | Parents & Siblings

Born Sarah Jane Jones in Florida, the Alabama alum is the second of three children to Gordon Jones and Holly Jones.

She has an older brother named Will Jones, a former soccer player at Mercer University, and a younger brother Mac Jones, an NFL player.

Sarah’s exact date of birth is not out, but her birthday is on July 10- her NFL star brother wishes her birthday on his social media. As of December 2023, she is in her late 20s.

Sports enthusiasm is in the blood of the Jones family.

Her father Gordon Jones played tennis at Florida State University and Flagler College. While at Flager, he led the team to the 1977 NAIA Championship victory and later was inducted into the Flagler Athletics Hall of Fame in 2004.

Even more, Sarah’s father was featured in Sports Illustrated’s “Faces in the Crowd” section for his impressive achievements.

Her mother, Holly, also played tennis at Mercer University.

Now, after all these years, both of Sarah’s parents are in a law firm- they are partners in the firm Jones & McCorkle in Jacksonville, Florida.

Sarah Is Proud Sister Of NFL Star | More On Brothers!

While Sarah Jones followed her parent’s path to tennis, her brothers caught their interest in football.

Her older brother, Will, played soccer at Mercer University. Upon graduation, he attended medical school in “the islands,” continuing his dedication to learning and personal growth.

Mac Jones Sister | High School Career

Tennis

Sarah Jones had a successful and accomplished high school career at The Bolles School in Jacksonville, Florida.

As a member of the school’s tennis team, she achieved several impressive feats. For instance, Sarah won the state title for No. 3 singles as a sophomore and advanced to the state finals in No. 1 doubles.

Likewise, Sarah went undefeated during the regular season in her senior year. She also won regionals and districts and advanced to the state level.

Further, she was also a tennis team captain for two years, received the Coaches Award twice, and won the MVP award.

In addition to her tennis court achievements, Sarah was a USTA Team Tennis National Finalist in 2011 and a Semifinalist in 2012. Likewise, she earned a ranking as high as 25 in Florida from TennisRecruiting.net.

Other accomplishments

Outside of tennis, Sarah was also an accomplished cross-country runner. She lettered five times in varsity cross country. Likewise, she also won the state cross-country championships in 2009 and 2013.

Mac Jones’s sister was the captain of the cross-country team and received several accolades for her service and academic achievements.

For instance, she won the Van Etten Bent Award at her school three times for the athlete who contributes the most service while maintaining their grades. She also won the O.C. Garces Award for being the best female athlete with great service as a junior.

Sarah was also a member of the National Honor Society and served as the Secretary of the Chinese National Honor Society.

She consistently earned first and second honors throughout high school, demonstrating her dedication to athletics and academics.

In summary, Sarah’s high school career was marked by a strong commitment to her passions and a determination to excel in sports and academics.

Mac Jones Sister | College Career

Sarah Jones had a successful and accomplished career as a student-athlete at The College of Charleston.

Her college career was marked by a strong commitment to her sport, dedication to academics, and willingness to rise to the challenge in individual and team competitions.

Senior Year

As a senior in the 2017-2018 academic year, Sarah appeared in the CofC Classic and won the Flight Seven Singles crown with a victory over Samford’s Grace Jennings (6-7, 6-2, 10-7).

Likewise, she finished the season with a 3-1 record in singles play, primarily competing from the No. 6 position with a 4-5 record in doubles action. The young player played the majority of her matches alongside Sarah Zschunke.

Sarah’s recognition as one of six players in CofC history to earn four straight ITA Scholar-Athlete Awards demonstrated her dedication to her sport and academics. 

Junior Year

During her junior year (2016-17), Sarah competed in the 16th Annual CofC Invitational and split her singles matches, prevailing against Kelly Dodd of Charlotte in B2 Singles.

She played two doubles matches while pairing up with Sarah Zschunke in B1 Doubles and went 5-3 in dual singles play from the 5 and 6 positions.

Similarly, she achieved wins against Charlotte Madson of William & Mary (6-1, 6-4) and Sarjana Sudhir of Buffalo (6-3, 6-4), helping the Cougars to a pair of 4-3 victories.

Sarah also posted a 5-10 dual record in third-flight doubles play, including a 7-6(4) win against UMass to help the Cougars seal the doubles point.

Sophomore Year

As a sophomore (2015-16), Sarah teamed up with Sarah Zschunke to win the B1 Round Robin Tournament at the CofC Invitational with a perfect 3-0 record.

She finished the fall with a 3-3 doubles record and a 1-5 singles record and appeared in 27 of the 28 matches for the Cougars in the spring season.

Likewise, Mac Jones’ sister posted a 7-12 record in singles play during the spring, primarily competing in the sixth flight, and achieved a 6-1 singles record between March 4 and 14.

Sarah also pulled off a 5-8 record in doubles play, competing with Sarah Zschunke as her doubles partner in the third flight.

Freshman Year

During her freshman year (2014-15), Sarah was honored with the team’s Coaches Award. Further, she achieved her first collegiate singles win at the home opener against McKayla Zupan of Appalachian State, winning 7-5, 6-3.

Sarah had an impressive six-match win streak and was the only non-senior to win the singles against UMBC. She also rose to the challenge during her first CAA tournament match, winning her singles match against Drexel in the quarterfinals.
