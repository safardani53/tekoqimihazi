HBO cancels popular comedy after four seasons as network breaks silence on shocking decision

HBO has slashed another major show from its line-up amid the latest shakeup at the TV network.

Comedian Robin Thede's A Black Lady Sketch Show ran its course at HBO, getting axed after only a few seasons.

Showrunners of the Emmy-winning series have chosen to bring the show to an end following its fourth season.

A Black Lady Sketch Show won't return for season five, Variety reported on Thursday.

HBO execs divulged in a statement that their decision to cancel A Black Lady Sketch doesn't mean that their respect for the series or the show creator Robin, 43, has wavered.

"For four exceptional seasons of ‘A Black Lady Sketch Show’ we’ve been thrilled to partner with her on this groundbreaking, hilarious series,” HBO representatives said of Robin.

Read more on HBO

“Robin, alongside Hoorae, Jax Media, and the incredible cast and crew, leave an indelible legacy not only in sketch, but in television comedy, and we look forward to continued creative collaboration under her deal with us.”

Robin also spoke out about her show being cut at HBO in her own statement, thanking her "cast members, guest stars, and crew members" for producing such a quality show.

"Thank you to all the immensely talented cast members, guest stars, writers, directors, editors and crew members who have made ‘A Black Lady Sketch Show’ an Emmy-winning success,” Robin expressed.

“I especially want to recognize the Black women and other women of color who excelled in every department at ‘ABLSS.’

"I am also eternally grateful for the support of Issa Rae and Jax Media, Amy Gravitt, Casey Bloys and the entire team at HBO, Max and Warner Bros. Discovery.”

RAGING FANS

Fans on Twitter expressed their strong disapproval of HBO's latest move.

"NOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOO!!!! More. We want more," one fan whined.

Another fan posted a gif that read, "No! God please!"

One supporter directly slammed HBO Max CEO David Zaslav, 63, for ending A Black Lady Sketch Show.

"The fact that it was able to last as long as it did and survive the [David] Zaslav slaughter enough to have one more season is a miracle on to itself. It was a great show!," one fan ranted.

CUT!

Before it was announced that HBO would be cutting the comedy series, it was revealed that the network's higher-ups wouldn't be renewing Game Theory with Bonami Jones for a third season.

The show was believed to have seen a steep decline in viewership numbers after it debuted in 2022.

Game Theory promised to bring ESPN star Bomani Jones to "discuss timely issues that transcend the world of sports."

But each season, the series lost an average of 80 percent of its lead-in viewership, OutKick reports.

Despite the show's episodes re-airing on TNT in the second season, the airings reportedly often failed to rank in the top 150 cable chart.

Still, many Twitter users took to social media to express their disappointment over the show's cancellation.

One tweeted: "Aww this stinks. I love that show!!!!"

Another wrote: "Game Theory was a great show. Bomani is a brilliant analyst.

"This show was a desperately needed to balance out the common thoughtless ramblings that we hear on most of these other shows."

A third said: "End of an era. Was a great show IMO."
