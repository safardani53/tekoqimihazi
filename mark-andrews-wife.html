About Mark Andrews

The Baltimore Ravens tight end Mark Andrews was born on September 6, 1995, to his parents, Paul and Martha Andrews, in Scottsdale, Arizona.

The son of a urologist father and real-estate agent mother, Mark grew up with his siblings in Scottsdale. He attended Desert Mountain High School nearby his residence, where he played football mostly as a wide receiver.

After high school graduation, Mark enrolled in the University of Oklahoma, where he studied management major. Besides that, he played college football for the Oklahoma Sooners for three seasons.

Andrews left his academics to pursue an NFL career in 2018.

Mark entered the NFL after the Baltimore Ravens chose him as the 86th overall pick in the 2018 draft.

One of the best tight ends during his college, Andrews had won several awards for his performance for the Sooners, including the John Mackey Award and Ozzie Newsome Award in 2017.

He has continued his incredible performance for the Ravens in the NFL as well.

He has already appeared in two Pro Bowls in five seasons, nearly recording 300 receptions and over 30 receiving touchdowns.

Andrews was selected to the First-team All-Pro for his outstanding performance throughout the 2021 season.
