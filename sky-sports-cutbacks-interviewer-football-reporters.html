Sky Sports legend 'told to take huge pay cut to continue 31-year career as network brutally axes hal

LEGENDARY interviewer Geoff Shreeves has reportedly been asked to take a huge pay cut to stay amid swinging cuts at Sky Sports.

Six of the broadcaster's 13 football reporters have been axed from their positions in a major cull.

The Mail say Shreeves, 59, is weighing up his future after 31 years in his iconic tunnel role.

He joined Sky for the first Premier League season, 1992-193.

Now Shreeves might have to choose between redundancy or remaining with "significantly" lower wages.

SunSport revealed in March how Sky were pondering a brutal staff shake-up.

It's understood the company's football reporters have been consulted over pay-offs.

Some are said to have chosen voluntary redundancy, while others might be able to find different jobs within Sky.

The Mail name the six reporters not staying in their current role as Guy Havord, Dickie Davis, Bianca Westwood, Greg Whelan, Lynsey Hooper and Jaydee Dyer. 

Those staying on are listed as Emma Saunders, David Craig, Patrick Davidson, Jonathan Oakes, Johnny Phillips and Luke Shanley.
