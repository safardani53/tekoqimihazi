Who is Christine Sinclair?

Christine Sinclair, a Canadian professional soccer player, is currently the captain of the Canadian national team and Portland Thorns FC.

As stated earlier, Sinclair has scored the most international goals for men or women worldwide, with 190 goals.

Moreover, she is among the most-capped active international footballers, having played more than 300 matches.

Early Life & Family

Christine Sinclair was born on June 12, 1983, to Bill and Sandra Sinclair, both of whom were born in Vancouver. She grew up with her older brother, Mike Sinclair.

Her father, Bill Sinclair, and uncles, Brian and Bruce Gant, were all Canadian amateur soccer champions.

When Christine was four months old, her father, Bill, was the team manager and helped Vancouver Firefighters FC win the 1983 Canada Soccer National Championships in Thunder Bay.

Christine graduated from the University of Portland in 2005 with a degree in Life Science.

She received an Honorary Doctorate of law from Simon Fraser University in 2013.

Sadly, Christine’s father, Bill, passed away on April 16, 2016, less than four months before the Rio Olympics. Despite this emotional setback, she led the Canadian team to win the Olympic bronze medal.

Her mother, Sandra, died due to multiple sclerosis on February 2, 2022.

Professional Career

At 16, Christine Sinclair debuted with the senior national team during the 2000 Algarve Cup and scored her first international goal during the tournament.

Over the years, Sinclair has represented Canada at four Olympic Games, winning a gold medal at Tokyo 2020 after previously securing back-to-back bronze medals at London 2012 and Rio 2016.

Sinclair holds the record for being the captain of a team that has participated in the World Cup a record number of four times, and she has scored at least one goal in five different World Cups, sharing the record with Marta, which Cristiano Ronaldo later matched.

Sinclair has also published a book titled “Playing A Long Time,” which reflects on both her exhilarating successes and heartbreaking failures.

Furthermore, she and Alex Morgan were featured on the FIFA 16 video game cover alongside Lionel Messi.

Christine Sinclair Net Worth & Earnings

Christine Sinclair’s primary source of income is her career in professional football, which has helped her accumulate a significant net worth of around $2 million as of 2023.

Throughout her career, she has earned numerous accolades, including being named the Canadian Women’s Player of the Year a record 14 times.

In addition to her success on the field, Sinclair has secured various endorsement deals with Envision Sports & Entertainment and Mission Athlete Care, contributing to her net worth.
