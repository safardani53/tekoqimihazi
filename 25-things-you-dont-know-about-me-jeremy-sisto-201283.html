25 Things You Don't Know About Me: Jeremy Sisto

Jeremy Sisto, 37, (Suburgatory airs on ABC Wednesdays at 8:30 p.m.) shares the 25 things you don't know about him with Us Weekly.

1. My Native American name is Running Bear.

2. My dad is a jazz musician.

3. My brother-in-law is a cop.

4. I wore a Santa hat for a whole year in high school.

5. I was the poor kid at a rich school, and I used to bum money in the halls for candy.

6. I'm still mad at my seventh-grade baseball coach for benching me a whole season.

7. When I can't remember my lines, I start sweating profusely.

8. When I was born, I lived in a rock house [in the Sierra Nevada mountains] with no plumbing.

9. I slept in a phone booth in Europe when I was 19.

10. I once went to an audition in full Army fatigues and black war paint on my face.

11. I make my dentist give me nitrous oxide, even for a cleaning.

12. My first concert was Anne Murray — but I tell people it was Paul Simon.

13. My wife [Addie Lane] is an amazing horseback rider.

14. My favorite picture from her modeling days is from a trucker calendar.

15. My wife was voted "Girl you'd most like to have sex with" in [her L.A.] high school. (I don't want to raise my kids in L.A.)

16. Once during Six Feet Under, the crew couldn't find me because I was at a blackjack table.

17. I used to think acting was really difficult.

18. I started a porch-swing company. It's defunct.

19. My daughter, Chuck [2], likes it when I tickle her face.

20. I just put out my first CD as my alter ego, Escape Tailor.

21. I'm tired from my twenties.

22. I like honey in peanut butter.

23. I'm certain my wife has saved my life and will again.
