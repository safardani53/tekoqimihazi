GINA'S SOBRIETY JOURNEY

In April, Gina revealed to People that she is sober and how it changed her life.

"I can't begin to describe how much it's changed me," she told the outlet.

"Drinking was a big part of my life and to eliminate it was a huge shift for me personally. But it's had this trickle-down, ripple effect where I've just seen so many things shift for the better. And I couldn't be happier."

She decided to stop drinking over a year and a half ago.

Gina opened up about her sobriety journey on the fifth season of Real Housewives of Orange County.

The reality TV star realized she was becoming too dependent on alcohol, so she decided to make the change.

"I was drinking way too much. What used to be a drink when I was out at night with friends became, especially during COVID, me pouring a drink every night without even thinking about it," Gina told People.

"And I don't identify as an alcoholic, I don't believe I have that disease. But I felt the scales starting to tip, and I didn't like that feeling. I was numbing myself with it. I was self-medicating, and I couldn't stop. It was more in control of me than I was of it."

She continued to say that when she thought she could stop, she would have a hard week, and pick up a drink.

Gina then realized it was serious.
