Derrick Jones: Early Life and Family

Derrick Labrent Jones Jr. was born in Chester, Pennsylvania, on February 15, 1997. His father’s name is Derrick Jones Sr. Likewise, his mother’s name is Otiesse Jones.

The player has one brother named Lakeem Johnson. Similarly, he also has two sisters, Dej’sha Jones and On’Jesha Jones. 

He attended Archbishop John Carroll High School in Radnor, Pennsylvania. For his further education, he enrolled himself at the University of Nevada, Las Vegas. 

Derrick Jones: High School Career

In 2014–15, Derrick was voted Southeastern Pennsylvania Player of the Year, Delaware County Player of the Year, and PIAA Triple-A Player of the Year.

Moreover, his team was also the first-team All-Catholic League. Hence, Derrick had a great year as a senior at Archbishop John Carroll High School.

Archbishop Carroll went 23–7 overall, with a 12–3 record in conference play, under coach Paul Romanczuk in 2014–15. Derrick had 30 points, 18 rebounds, and five blocked shots in the state tournament semifinals.

He also averaged 19.2 points, 10.1 rebounds, and 2.3 blocks per game this season. During his high school career, the player scored 1,645 points, grabbed 1,029 rebounds, and blocked 268 shots.

Derrick Jones: College Career

In the 2015–16 NCAA season, Derrick joined the UNLV Runnin’ Rebels as a freshman.

The NCAA Eligibility Center asked that the ACT examine test results reported by Jones and many other minority student-athletes at a specific testing facility in Baltimore, Maryland, prior to the season’s commencement.

Despite this, Derrick was approved by the NCAA before UNLV’s first game in November versus Cal Poly.

Additionally, Derrick was the second-leading scorer for the Runnin’ Rebels in 2015–16. In 30 games, he averaged 11.5 points on 58.9 percent shooting and 4.5 rebounds.

During the season, the player had four double-doubles, including a career-high 26 points and 10 rebounds versus Chaminade in the Maui Invitational Tournament. 

On February 29, 2016, the ACT testing agency told UNLV that Derrick’s score had been annulled. As a result, Jones was ruled ineligible to compete by the NCAA and was forced to miss the final three games of UNLV’s season. 

Derrick Jones: Professional Career

Derrick declared for the 2016 NBA draft on April 7, 2016. However, Jones did not hire an agent and left open the possibility of returning to UNLV or attending another collegiate program.

Later, Jones acquired an agent and stayed in the draft. The player was undrafted in the 2016 NBA Draft and then joined the Sacramento Kings for the 2016 NBA Summer League.

Unfortunately, Derrick was unable to participate in the Kings throughout the tournament due to a groin ailment. The player signed a training camp contract with the Phoenix Suns on September 25, 2016.

He earned a place on the Suns’ opening-night roster after impressing the team during training camp and the preseason.

The player was acquired by the Northern Arizona Suns on December 12, 2017. However, the deal did not last long. Derrick signed a two-way deal with the Miami Heat on December 31, 2017.

Jones, who was signed to a two-way deal, spent his time between the Heat and its NBA G League franchise, the Sioux Falls Skyforce.

On January 9, 2018, he got his first start for the Heat in only his third game, earning eight points, five rebounds, one assist, and two blocks in 28 minutes against the Toronto Raptors.

Likewise, Derrick signed a two-year, $3.2 million standard deal with the Heat on July 1, 2018. 

Jones agreed to terms with the Portland Trail Blazers on a two-year, $19 million contract on November 22, 2020. 

He was acquired by the Chicago Bulls on August 28, 2021, along with a first and second-round selection in a three-team sign-and-trade that also included the Cleveland Cavaliers.

Derrick Jones: Injury

Like all the other NBA players, Derrick Jones is also not new to injury. The player’s performance has time and again been interrupted by various injuries.

Derrick suffered a right knee injury on January 12, 2022, during a 112–138 defeat to the Brooklyn Nets.

The Bulls stated the next day that he had a bone bruise and would be out for at least 4-to-6 weeks, however that timeframe was later revised to 2-to-4 weeks.

The Bulls announced on January 26 that Jones had broken his right index finger during a workout and will be out for another 6-to-8 weeks.
