Who is Amanda Anisimova?

Amanda Anisimova is an American-Russian Tennis player.

She has the highest WTA ranking of No. 21 in the world, and since just before she turned 17, she has consistently been in the top 100. Anisimova has made three WTA finals and won two WTA Tour titles.

Anisimova started playing tennis at a very young age and has had her father Konstantin as her long-time coach. Her older sister is also a tennis player.

When Anisimova was very young, her parents made the decision to relocate to Florida in order to provide their kids with better training opportunities.

Anisimova achieved a world ranking of No. 2 as a junior. She won two other Grade-A titles in addition to the girls’ singles title at the 2017 US Open.

Early Life of Amanda Anisimova

Amanda Anisimova was born on August 31, 2001, in Freehold Township, New Jersey.

Olga Anisimova and Konstantin Anisimov gave birth to Amanda Anisimova. Maria, her older sister, attended Wharton’s undergraduate business school and competed in college tennis for the University of Pennsylvania.

A few years before she was born, her parents moved from Russia to the United States in order to provide their older daughter with better opportunities.

They both worked in the banking and finance sectors, and neither of them grew up playing competitive tennis.

Her starting Tennis

At age five, Anisimova began playing tennis. She attributes her inspiration for taking up the sport to her sister, stating, “She was playing tennis when I was a child.

I wanted to play because I saw her playing all the time. That is how both my parents and I got involved in it.”

When she was a young child, her family relocated to Florida so that Amanda and her sister would have more opportunities to train and network with other coaches.

When she was a junior, her father served as her primary coach for a while, and her mother also assisted in her coaching. She had also collaborated with Nick Saviano since she was 11 years old.

Amanda Anisimova Career

In 2016, Anisimova ranked No. 2 globally in the ITF junior rankings, which was a career-high.

Early in her junior career, she was ranked outside the top 300 when she competed in the 2015 Abierto Juvenil Mexicano, but she unexpectedly won the Grade A tournament at the age of 14.

She maintained her high standards in 2016, winning the Grade 1 Copa del Café and competing in the Grade A Copa Gerdau final.

Anisimova was the No. 2 seed at the French Open based on these outcomes.

She competed in the second junior Grand Slam tournament of her career, becoming the first American to reach the girls’ final since Ashley Harkleroad in 2002 before falling to Rebeka Masarova.

She participated in the USTA Girls’ 18s National Championship during the summer.

Anisimova won two more significant championships at the age of 15, the first at the Grade 1 Yucatán Cup in late 2016 and the second at the Grade A Copa Gerdau in early 2017, where she had placed third the previous year.

She participated in just two more ITF junior tournaments that year after winning these titles, both of which were Grand Slams.

She won her first Grand Slam championship at the US Open to cap off her junior career, defeating fellow American Coco Gauff in the championship match while going undefeated overall.

Anisimova was a member of the American team that won the Junior Fed Cup in 2017, but she was unable to compete in the championship match due to illness.
